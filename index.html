About Baguio, Philippines

Baguio City, located in the northern Philippines in Benguet province, is known for its stunning mountainous setting. Positioned around 250 kilometers to the north of Manila, the capital, it stands out for its cool climate attributed to its high altitude of 1,540 meters above sea level. Referred to as the "Summer Capital of the Philippines," the city boasts a subtropical highland climate where temperatures seldom rise above 26 degrees Celsius. This unique weather pattern attracts a diverse range of visitors, both local and international, looking to enjoy a break from the sweltering tropical conditions prevalent in much of the country.

Festivals in Baguio City

Here are some of the festivals celebrated in Baguio City, showcasing its vibrant cultural scene and community spirit.

Panagbenga Festival

Panagbenga Festival: February festival in Baguio City celebrating its cultural heritage with a grand parade of flower-decorated floats and cultural performances.

Ibagiw Creative Festival

Ibagiw Creative Festival: An October to November event showcasing local artists and artisans through exhibits, performances, and workshops, celebrating Baguio City's heritage and culture.

Mangan Taku Food Festival

Mangan Taku Food Festival: April event honoring Baguio's culinary traditions with culinary competitions, food crawls, and sampling of dishes from Cordillera provinces, accompanied by local music performances.
